The Problem

Most Insoles Only Provide Cushioning, Not Real Support

Standard foam and gel insoles are designed for comfort — but they do nothing to control harmful foot motion. For people recovering from injuries, managing chronic conditions, or living with limited mobility, cushioning alone is not enough.

Custom orthotics can provide real structural support — but they cost $400–$800, require multiple specialist visits, and aren't accessible to most people. This leaves a significant gap for patients who need medical-grade stabilization but can't afford or access custom solutions.

The Science

Why Carbon Fiber Works

Carbon fiber is the same material used in aerospace, prosthetics, and surgical devices. Its stiffness-to-weight ratio allows Stabrix insoles to redistribute ground forces and limit damaging motion without adding bulk.

01

Force Redistribution

Carbon fiber's rigidity redirects compressive forces across a broader surface area, reducing focal pressure points that cause pain and slow healing.

02

Motion Control

Controlled rigidity limits excessive pronation and supination — the two primary biomechanical contributors to foot, ankle, and knee injuries during recovery.

03

Long-Lasting Support

Unlike foam or gel that degrades in weeks, carbon fiber maintains structural integrity over 18–24 months — providing consistent support throughout your entire recovery.
